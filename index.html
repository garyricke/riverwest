01 — Who We Are

A real gym.
There aren't
many left.

River West has been at 108 First Street since the late 1980s. Chris Hylton has owned it since 2003 — not because it was a business plan, but because he refused to let a great community gym disappear.

When Delnor Hospital decided to shut it down, Chris bought it. He'd been training the hospital president's family. He knew the members. He knew what the place meant to the neighborhood.

He's trained thousands of people since — professional athletes, stay-at-home parents, Division I players, an Olympic equestrian, and 80-year-old men who could barely get from their car to the front door. He knows the patterns. He knows what works.

"This is your new job. It's never ending — and that's the point."

02 — What We Stand For

Three things.
Nothing else.

STRENGTH

Physical capability built through real training with real weight. Full-size bars, Olympic plates, free weights up to any level you're at. Not machines. Not simulations.

LONGEVITY

The goal isn't a short-term result. Core strength, leg strength, range of motion — these determine your quality of life at 60, 70, and beyond. That's what we work on.

COMMUNITY

A place where people know each other and hold each other accountable. The owner knows your name because he actually wants to know it — not because it's in a script.

03 — How It Works

Three ways in.

THE GYM

Membership

Two floors. Free weights, machines, cardio, functional equipment — everything a serious person needs. Full-size bars. Sleds. Rowers. Ski ergs. Tires. Jacob's Ladders. If you need it for a real workout, it's here.

Come in. Work hard. Do your thing. No one's going to pressure you into anything the moment you walk in the door. The people who join here tend to come — and that's by design.

BOOT CAMP

4 Classes Per Week

Weights on Monday. Cardio and plyometrics on Wednesday. Core and mixed work on Thursday. Full-body interval training on Saturday at 9 AM. Chris programs it. Chris leads it. The classes are not gentle.

Eight sessions is the threshold. Get through eight and something shifts — you start gauging yourself, you see progress, the work becomes its own motivation. Start with Saturday. The interval format means you go at whatever pace you can sustain.

"It's brutal. It's also amazing — but only because it's brutal."

PERSONAL TRAINING

One-on-One with Chris

Not hand-holding. A plan — built around what you actually need, pushed to what you can actually handle, sustainable enough to build on. Thirty minutes of focused work, an hour total including warm-up and finishing cardio.

The goal isn't a client who needs a trainer forever. It's a client who learns enough in ten sessions to train on their own. Ten sessions is enough to build a foundation, learn the movements, and understand your own baselines.

"I do the thinking for you. You show up, you do what I tell you."

More about Chris
